A seating study that explores the tension between industrial process and domestic warmth. The structural shell is formed from a single sheet of 2mm cold-rolled steel, folded and welded, then powder-coated in a warm off-white.

## Concept

The brief to myself was simple: make a chair that disappears. Not literally — but one that doesn't announce itself when you walk into a room.

### Process

Early prototypes explored pure sheet metal forms, but the result felt too utilitarian. Introducing the oak seat and backrest resolved the coldness without compromising the structural logic.

### Challenges

Tolerances on the folded joints were tighter than expected — a 0.4mm deviation at the bend radius changed the whole visual rhythm of the legs. The final jig took three iterations to get right.
